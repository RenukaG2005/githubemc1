Devour Barcelona Tapas & Wine Tour in El Born Neighborhood

Tickets & Tours
Duration: 3h

Enjoy an indulgent introduction to Barcelona’s gourmet delights, all while you explore the atmospheric El Born neighborhood, on this small-group tour. In the evening, meet your guide in the area, and come ready with an appetite: you’ll visit three different tapas bars, each of which offers its own specialties. As you go, learn more about Barcelona’s culinary traditions, and sample five different Spanish wines to accompany your bites.


Playa de la Mar Bella

Beaches
Duration: 1-2 hours

Created for the 1992 Summer Olympics, this city beach is a lively spot that's especially popular amongst younger locals and travelers. You can sunbathe on the beach or swim in the cool waters, and even play some beach volleyball, basketball, and table tennis at the facilities provided. Water sports lovers can also visit the nautical center at the western end for sailing or windsurfing. There's even a designated nudist area, one of the only ones in the city. End your day at the beach after going on a coastal tour of Barcelona, which might pass by Playa de La Barceloneta and Basilica de la Sagrada Familia. – Tripadvisor

Barcelona Casa Milà - La Pedrera

Architectural Buildings
Duration: 1-2 hours

Casa Mila, popularly known as La Pedrera, is a most unusual building, constructed between 1906 and 1912 by the architect Antoni Gaudí (1852–1926) and declared UNESCO World Heritage in 1984. Today it is the headquarters of Fundacio Catalunya La Pedrera and houses a cultural centre that is a reference point in Barcelona for the range of activities it organises and the different spaces for exhibitions and other public uses it contains. A visit to La Pedrera, landmark building and container, gives us a better understanding and appreciation of architecture and transports us to the period when Antoni Gaudi lived.
